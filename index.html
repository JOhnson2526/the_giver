About the Book

The Giver
Lois Lowry
1993

" The Giver is a 1993 American young adult dystopian novel written by Lois Lowry. It is set in a society which at first appears to be utopian but is revealed to be dystopian as the story progresses.

The novel follows 12-year-old Jonas, who is selected to become the Receiver of Memory, a role that grants him access to memories of the past that are kept hidden from the rest of society. As Jonas learns more about the true nature of his community, he faces difficult choices that challenge everything he has been taught.

The Giver won the 1994 Newbery Medal and has sold over 12 million copies worldwide. It is the first book in The Giver Quartet, followed by Gathering Blue, Messenger, and Son.

About Lois Lowry

Lois Lowry is one of America's most respected and versatile children's book authors. She has written more than 40 books for young adults, spanning genres from contemporary fiction to historical fiction, dystopian fiction, and poetry.

Lowry's writing often explores complex themes such as racism, terminal illness, murder, and the Holocaust, challenging young readers to think deeply about difficult subjects. Her experiences as a military child, moving frequently and living in different countries, have influenced her writing and perspective.

The inspiration for The Giver came from Lowry's father, who was losing his memory. This experience made her wonder about a society without memory and how that would affect relationships and humanity. Her books continue to be widely read and studied in schools around the world.

Main Characters

Key individuals in The Giver

Jonas

The Protagonist

Jonas is the 12-year-old protagonist of the story. He is selected to become the new Receiver of Memory, a prestigious but lonely position in his community.

As Jonas receives memories from the Giver, he begins to question the society in which he lives and develops a strong sense of empathy for others.

His character undergoes a profound transformation as he learns about love, pain, joy, and the importance of memory and individuality.

The Giver

The Memory Keeper

The Giver is the current Receiver of Memory who trains Jonas. He carries the burden of all the community's memories, both painful and joyful.

He is wise, patient, and understands the importance of memories in shaping human experience. Over time, he becomes a mentor and friend to Jonas.

The Giver believes that the community needs to change and supports Jonas in his decision to leave.

Gabriel

The Infant

Gabriel is a newchild (infant) who comes to stay with Jonas's family temporarily. He is small and has trouble sleeping through the night.

Jonas forms a special bond with Gabriel and discovers that the infant also has the ability to receive memories. Gabriel becomes a symbol of innocence and hope.

Later in the story, Jonas's love for Gabriel becomes a driving force behind his decision to escape the community.

Fiona

Jonas's Friend

Fiona is Jonas's close friend who is assigned to be a Caretaker of the Old. She is kind, gentle, and has red hair, which Jonas only sees when he begins to receive memories.

Jonas has feelings for Fiona, though he doesn't fully understand them until he learns about love through the memories.

Her character represents the potential for beauty and emotion that exists even within the constraints of the community.

Asher

Jonas's Best Friend

Asher is Jonas's fun-loving best friend who often makes silly mistakes and has difficulty with precision of language.

He is assigned to be the Assistant Director of Recreation, which suits his playful personality.

As Jonas's perspective changes, he begins to see the limitations in Asher's adherence to the community's rules and lack of depth.

Lily

Jonas's Sister

Lily is Jonas's 7-year-old sister. She is talkative, curious, and still learning the rules of the community.

Her character represents the innocence of childhood within the community and the way children are socialized to accept its norms without question.

Lily looks forward to receiving her bicycle and her front-buttoned jacket, important milestones in the community.
